Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Halo Ring

Everything you need to know about the Halo Ring and how it works.

The Halo Ring uses NFC technology to enable contactless payments. The ring contains a secure NFC chip that stores your payment information. When you want to make a payment, simply tap your ring on any contactless payment terminal, just like you would with a contactless card. The ring connects to the HaloPay app on your smartphone, allowing you to manage your payment methods and track your transactions.

Yes! The Halo Ring has an IP68 rating, which means it is water-resistant in fresh water to a maximum depth of 1.5 meters for up to 30 minutes. You can wear it while washing your hands, swimming in a pool, or taking a shower without any issues. However, we recommend avoiding saltwater and high-pressure water streams.

The Halo Ring comes with a dedicated charging case that provides wireless charging. Simply place your ring in the charging case, and it will begin charging automatically. With a 33 mAh battery and 15 watts charging capability, it takes only 5-10 minutes to fully charge your ring. A full charge typically lasts several days, depending on usage.

Absolutely! The Halo Ring uses bank-level encryption and tokenization technology to ensure your payment information is secure. Each transaction generates a unique code, meaning your actual card details are never shared. Additionally, the HaloPay app requires authentication before linking payment methods to your ring, and you can instantly deactivate your ring from the app if it's ever lost or stolen.

The Halo Ring is compatible with most major debit and credit cards, including Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover. You can link multiple cards to your HaloPay app and select which one to use as the default payment method for your ring. The HaloPay app also supports digital wallets like Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay.
